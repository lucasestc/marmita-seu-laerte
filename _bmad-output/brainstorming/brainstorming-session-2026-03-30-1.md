## Ideas Generated — Phase 1: What If Scenarios

### Product

**[Product #1]: The Marmita Subscription**
_Concept:_ Weekly or monthly pre-paid plan — workers subscribe to receive the daily fixed menu. Prepayment tells Dad exactly how many marmitas to cook, zero waste, zero guessing.
_Novelty:_ Removes the daily friction of deciding lunch. The marmita just exists in your day, like your morning coffee.

**[Product #2]: The Weekly Menu Reveal**
_Concept:_ Every Sunday evening (or Friday), the app sends the full next week's menu — all 5 days, dishes listed clearly with ingredients highlighted for common allergens. Subscribers know their whole week ahead of time and can pause a specific day if needed.
_Novelty:_ Full control for the customer while maintaining the daily ritual of the story. The anticipation builds across the week.

**[Product #3]: The Daily Story Notification**
_Concept:_ Morning push notification isn't a reveal — it's a love letter to the dish. "Hoje é escondidinho de carne seca. O pai prepara desde as 7h da manhã. Receita da família, manteiga na massa, queijo coalho por cima." You already know what's coming. The story makes you hungrier for it.
_Novelty:_ Emotional marketing without anxiety. Amplifies anticipation, not uncertainty.

**[Product #4]: The Dietary Flag**
_Concept:_ During signup, users flag their restrictions (gluten, lactose, nuts, etc.). The weekly menu automatically highlights which days are safe for them — no manual checking needed.
_Novelty:_ Turns a safety concern into a trust signal — "this service actually knows me."

**[Product #5]: The Daily Rating**
_Concept:_ After lunch (1pm push notification), subscribers get a dead-simple rating prompt — one tap, 1–5 stars, optional emoji or short comment. Dad gets a weekly dashboard showing which dishes score highest, which days have the most skips/pauses, and trends over time.
_Novelty:_ Running on data from day one — a competitive advantage most restaurants don't have even after years.

**[Product #6]: The Menu Intelligence Loop**
_Concept:_ Low-rated dishes get quietly rotated out. High-rated dishes become "fan favorites" that return by popular demand — announced in the app. "Vocês pediram, o pai ouviu — escondidinho volta na quarta-feira."
_Novelty:_ Turns Dad's intuition into a community-driven menu. Subscribers feel ownership. Much harder to cancel when you helped build the menu.

**[Product #7]: The Ratings as Social Proof**
_Concept:_ Aggregate ratings displayed publicly on the app/landing page. "Média 4.8 ⭐ em 847 refeições servidas." Not a review platform — just one honest number that builds trust for new subscribers.
_Novelty:_ Converts every meal into a marketing asset automatically.

### Operations

**[Ops #1]: The Lobby Ambassador**
_Concept:_ Dad arrives at a fixed time (11:45am) and stands at the building entrance with a thermal bag and a list. Subscribers come down, grab their marmita in 30 seconds, go back up.
_Novelty:_ Dad's face IS the brand. People see colleagues picking up, get curious, ask "what is that?" — organic marketing built into the ops model. ✅ Selected as Phase 1 approach.

**[Ops #2]: The Floor Captain** _(future scenario — when companies pay)_
_Concept:_ One person per floor/company receives all marmitas and distributes to colleagues. Their subscription is discounted or free in exchange.
_Novelty:_ Turns a logistical constraint into a viral referral engine inside the building.

**[Ops #3b]: The Building Partnership**
_Concept:_ Approach building management for a formal "food service partner" arrangement — authorized vendor badge, daily entry window (11:45am–12:30pm). Buildings benefit as a tenant perk.
_Novelty:_ Not a delivery guy sneaking in — an authorized vendor. Signals legitimacy and trust.

**Launch sequence:** Lobby Ambassador → Building authorizes entry after seeing demand → Companies pay as team perk (Ops #2 territory)

**[Ops #4]: WhatsApp as the MVP Platform**
_Concept:_ Before the app is ready, orders and payments run entirely via WhatsApp — customers send their order, pay via Pix, get a confirmation message.
_Novelty:_ WhatsApp IS the app for most Brazilians. Zero dev cost, deeply familiar. Transitions naturally to app when ready.

**[Ops #5]: Get Credenciado Early**
_Concept:_ Register with main VR networks (Alelo, Sodexo, Ticket at minimum) via a maquininha from Stone, PagSeguro or similar — one of the first things to do before launch. Requires CNPJ.
_Novelty:_ Table stakes for Faria Lima — not a differentiator but a hard requirement.

**[Ops #6]: The Weekly Tap**
_Concept:_ Instead of automatic monthly billing, subscribers tap their VR card once a week at pickup on Mondays. Manual but works within VR's limitations.
_Novelty:_ Turns a limitation into a touchpoint — Monday tap becomes part of the ritual.

**[Ops #7]: Split Payment Model**
_Concept:_ Subscription billed weekly via VR at pickup. Monthly billing via Pix or credit card. Two subscription tiers by payment method.
_Novelty:_ Lets VR users subscribe without friction while keeping monthly Pix option open.

**[Ops #8 revised]: The VR Landscape Survey**
_Concept:_ During flyer distribution phase — before cooking a single marmita — ask people directly which VR platform they use. 20 answers gives a clear picture of which credenciamentos to prioritize.
_Novelty:_ Market research for free, built into the pre-launch conversation.

**[Ops #9]: MEI as the Minimum Viable Business**
_Concept:_ MEI costs ~R$70–75/month, gives a CNPJ immediately, covers food businesses under the right CNAE code, revenue ceiling R$81k/year. Unlocks VR credenciamento and maquininha.
_Novelty:_ At R$30/marmita, just 2–3 sales/month covers the MEI cost. The VR access it unlocks is worth far more.

### Marketing

**[Marketing #1]: The Lobby as a Living Ad**
_Concept:_ Daily pickup moment is deliberately designed as visible, aromatic, social — thermal bag branded, Dad in a simple apron with the logo, maybe a small "experimente hoje?" sign for walk-in trials.
_Novelty:_ Zero ad spend. The product demos itself daily to hundreds of hungry office workers.

**[Marketing #2]: The First Week Free Trial**
_Concept:_ Non-subscribers can grab an available marmita at walk-in price — only if there's stock left after subscribers are served. Scarcity built in.
_Novelty:_ Subscribers always have priority. Trying once feels like getting lucky — which makes subscribing feel like "securing your spot."

**[Marketing #3]: The Pre-Launch Hunger Flyer**
_Concept:_ Well-designed flyer with photos of dishes, weekly menu, price, and QR code to WhatsApp. Handed personally by Dad (or Lucas) at the lobby before a single marmita is cooked.
_Suggested content:_ Photo of dish, weekly menu, price, subscription option, tagline ("Feito em casa, entregue com carinho"), QR code → WhatsApp pre-typed message, photo of Dad.
_Novelty:_ It's not an ad — it's a menu. People keep it because it's useful.

**[Marketing #4]: The Card That Does the Selling** _(applied to flyer)_
_Concept:_ QR code on the flyer opens WhatsApp with a pre-typed first message ("Olá, vi o cardápio e quero saber mais!") — barrier to first contact is one tap, not typing.
_Novelty:_ Removes the "I'll look it up later" friction that means never.

**[Marketing #5]: The Founder's Face**
_Concept:_ Dad distributes the flyers himself, in his apron, with a warm "bom dia." The cook himself — not a promoter. People immediately associate the face with the food.
_Novelty:_ No restaurant chain can replicate this. An unfair advantage that only works at small scale — use it hard while you have it.

### Pricing

**[Pricing #1]: The Faria Lima Value Anchor**
_Concept:_ Typical Faria Lima lunch runs R$45–80+ (kilo) or R$60–120 (sit-down). At R$30–40, positioning isn't "budget lunch" — it's "exceptional value." The emotional premium (homemade, nostalgic) justifies R$40 easily.
_Novelty:_ Not competing on price, winning on value perception.

**[Pricing #2]: The Subscription Discount**
_Concept:_ Single order = R$35–40. Weekly subscription (5 days) = R$30/day. Monthly = R$28/day. Discount rewards commitment and locks in production planning.
_Novelty:_ Casual buyers pay full price. Loyal subscribers are rewarded. Cash flow is predictable.
_Note:_ Costs (ingredients + packaging) need formal calculation — target ~R$10–12/marmita for healthy margin.

### Brand

**[Brand #1]: The Irreplaceable Recipe**
_Concept:_ The taste IS the product. Dad's specific way of making arroz, feijão, frango — 40+ years of a person's cooking. Nobody can replicate it. Every competitor starts from zero.
_Novelty:_ iFood can copy the app. A competitor can copy the lobby approach. Nobody can copy Dad's mão na massa.

**[Brand #2]: The Named Cook**
_Concept:_ The cook's name IS the brand. Every flyer, WhatsApp message, and app screen leads with the person, not the product. Creates emotional bond that survives price competition entirely.
_Novelty:_ People aren't canceling their "Seu Laerte" subscription to save R$5.

**[Brand #3]: Name Options** _(to be discussed with Laerte)_
- "Do Seu Laerte" — warm, personal, exactly how Brazilians talk about a good cook
- "Marmita do Seu Laerte" — full and clear for launch
- "Feito pelo Laerte" — puts the craft front and center
- "De Oliveira" — family name, more brandable, scales better when other cooks join
- "Cozinha Oliveira" — restaurant feel while staying personal
- **"Do Oliveira para você"** — _(Lucas's idea, saved for later discussion)_

**Possible two-phase brand path:** Launch as "Marmita do Seu Laerte" → evolve to "Cozinha Oliveira" as scale grows

**Scale model:** Growth based on Laerte training new cooks on his exact recipes — not random hiring. The recipes stay in the family.

## Platform Architecture — Decided

### Decision: Web App from Day 1 (Option B)

Build a simple web app immediately — no WhatsApp-only MVP phase. Designed to scale cleanly without painful migrations.

---

### Tech Stack

| Layer | Technology | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Frontend + Backend | **Next.js** | Full-stack, fast to build, BMad-friendly |
| Database | **Supabase** | Postgres, real-time, easy auth integration |
| Payments | **Mercado Pago** | Pix native, credit card, Brazilian market standard |
| WhatsApp | **Z-API** | Brazilian service, ~R$50/month, REST API, no Meta approval needed |
| Auth | **Custom OTP via Z-API** | Phone number + 6-digit code sent via WhatsApp |

---

### Auth Flow

1. User enters phone number on login screen
2. Z-API sends WhatsApp message: "Seu código de acesso: 483921"
3. User types 6-digit code → logged in
4. Supabase stores session (30-day cookie)
5. Successive visits → already logged in, no action needed
6. Session expires → repeat phone + OTP flow

**Why phone OTP over email magic link:** More secure (harder to accidentally share), native to Brazilian user behavior, Z-API already in stack for notifications — no extra cost.
